My Team and Trading on Amazon

Everything a person needs, except for health, is sold. And in the USA, besides food, everything is sold on Amazon. There is a separate service in the company called Amazon Prime, it costs $140 a year - free two-day delivery throughout the year! More than 200 million people use this service. Just think about how active trading is happening on Amazon.

The total sales volume on Amazon exceeds $450 billion PER YEAR! Two-thirds of these sales are accounted for by private entrepreneurs who have business accounts on Amazon. There are thousands of courses and tools in the world that help start a business on Amazon. Most of them are just pushing the air to make some money on the incredible wave of the company's success, which has been going on for more than 10 years. Only a small part understands how the business on Amazon actually works.

Let me share my vision:

    1. Amazon has reached such heights in sales by following a fundamental rule: The customer is always RIGHT! The entire system is structured and set up in a way that primarily protects the interests of the end consumer.

    2. The best service always wins. All conditions are created for both businesses and end customers.

    3. Technology and digitization. Minimizing human intervention. Everything that can be automated is optimized.

So, in Amazon, there are only two types of organic sales. Through Amazon's warehouse - FBA. Through another warehouse - FBM.

A multi-million flow of customers is provided for tens of thousands of types of products. Each product has its category, its brand, and its product card (LISTING). Each category has its own specific features, each brand is protected, and each listing has its popularity (BSR, reviews) and its owner.

In general, product manufacturers focus only on production and have their distributors. These distributors have their B2B platforms in the form of a website, where they attract sellers of various levels. Some sell offline, and some sell online on Amazon or other marketplaces. They do not grant access to their product database to every seller. The U.S. economy is heavily regulated, so every movement of goods is documented through legal entities. Legal entities are also encouraged with high trust and large credits, and, consequently, significant opportunities are available specifically for legal entities. A supplier of goods can be considered an entity that can issue an invoice for the goods (Invoice) - a Legal Entity. ***Understanding this industry and being active in this field provides significant opportunities and high stability. This can be called "Behind the Scenes of Trading on Amazon."

But Amazon also allows individuals to work, offering the opportunity to launch their own product manufacturing under their own brand (Private Label). This path involves significant competition, and even experienced Amazon sellers approach PL launches very cautiously. The company also allows intermediaries to earn money through various methods (Drop, Flip, Arbitrage) by finding buyers for different products and delivering the goods directly from the supplier. There are various schemes, loopholes, and ways to make money along this path. At first glance, it may seem easy, but not everyone can earn in the long run. Since this niche is actively controlled by Amazon, there is even a comprehensive set of rules under the name of Dropshipping Policy. There are quite frequent exceptions where people have been able to make easy and substantial profits using these strategies. And these cases attract the attention of the majority. It's important to remember that exceptions are just that - exceptions, and they are strictly confidential.
